# Athlete-State Overlay — High-Capacity / Durability-Limited Return

Status: reusable overlay / default.
Profile type: athlete-state.
Profile id: high-capacity-durability-limited-return.

## Objective and main limiter

- Preserve high aerobic / total-load capacity while rebuilding specific mechanical durability.
- Main limiter: current tissue and impact tolerance, not the aerobic engine.

## Primary specificity axis

- Bias the plan around the modality that currently governs durable race readiness.
- In running-led builds, that often means running load and its associated long-duration mechanical cost.

## Hard-session priority order

- Protect the most mechanically expensive specific session first.
- Keep strong aerobic quality purposeful but not dense.
- Treat sharp intensity as supporting only unless the event overlay makes a stronger case.

## Spacing / density preferences

- Avoid clustering meaningful mechanical stress.
- Protect the recovery space around the most specific mechanical session of the block.
- Allow lower-impact support modalities when they preserve structure rather than hiding fatigue.

## Progression priorities

- Let total load rise faster than specific mechanical load when necessary.
- Stage long-duration progression carefully.
- Use support modalities to preserve aerobic load while the specific modality catches up.

## Alert sensitivity / default responses

- Treat oversized jumps in specific load, worsening next-day mechanics, and hidden density as high-salience alerts.
- Default response: hold specific-load progression, freeze the implicated long-duration progression, and redirect the next key stress toward lower-impact support if needed.

## Explicit non-goals

- Do not treat high total load as proof that specific durability is already restored.
- Do not chase hero days to prove robustness.
- Do not let quality density outrun current tissue tolerance.
